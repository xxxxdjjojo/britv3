1. Description
This Epic focuses on developing a suite of tools for Landlords and Estate Agents to efficiently manage their rental property portfolios. This includes features for tenant onboarding and management, handling maintenance requests (including integration with the service provider marketplace), tracking rental finances, managing property-related documents, and ensuring compliance with UK rental regulations.

2. Goals
To provide Landlords and Estate Agents with a centralized system to manage their rental properties.
To streamline tenant lifecycle management from application to move-out.
To simplify the process of handling and tracking property maintenance.
To offer tools for basic financial tracking and reporting related to rental income and expenses.
To help users stay compliant with essential rental regulations and document renewals.
3. Scope
In Scope:

Property Portfolio Management:
Ability for Landlords/Agents to add and manage details of their rental properties within their portfolio.
View overview of all managed properties with key status indicators (e.g., Vacant, Occupied, Maintenance Required).
Tenant Onboarding & Management:
System for managing rental applications (received via listings or manually added).
Integration points/guidance for tenant screening (e.g., links to third-party referencing services; Britestate itself won't perform checks initially).
Storage for tenant details and lease agreement information.
Basic digital lease agreement template generation (customizable fields).
Tracking of lease start/end dates and renewal reminders.
Maintenance Management:
System for Landlords/Agents to log and track maintenance issues reported by tenants (or proactively).
Ability to assign maintenance jobs to verified service providers from the Britestate marketplace (Epic 4).
Tracking of maintenance job status (e.g., Reported, Assigned, In Progress, Awaiting Invoice, Completed).
Storage of maintenance history per property.
Basic Financial Management (for rentals):
Manual tracking of rent payments received (mark as paid/overdue).
System for logging property-related expenses (e.g., repairs, service charges, insurance).
Simple financial overview per property (income vs. expenses).
Document Management (property & tenancy specific):
Secure storage for important documents linked to specific properties or tenancies (e.g., safety certificates, lease agreements, inspection reports).
Automated reminders for key document expiry dates (e.g., Gas Safety Certificate, EPC).
Compliance Management (Basic):
Checklist or guidance on common UK rental compliance requirements.
Linking document storage to compliance items.
Out of Scope:

Direct online rent payment collection via payment gateways (will be part of Epic 8).
Advanced financial accounting or tax calculation features.
Automated tenant communication sequences (beyond basic notifications).
Full property inspection scheduling and reporting tools (manual document upload is fine).
Direct integration with accounting software (e.g., Xero, QuickBooks).

5. Acceptance Criteria (General for the Epic)
Landlords and Agents can create and manage a portfolio of their rental properties.
Tenant application and lease information can be managed within the system.
Maintenance issues can be logged, tracked, and assigned to service providers from the marketplace.
Basic tracking of rental income and property expenses is functional.
Property and tenancy-related documents can be securely stored, with reminders for renewals.
The system provides guidance or checklists for common UK rental compliance requirements.
All user stories within this Epic meet their individual acceptance criteria.
6. Dependencies
Epic 1: Platform Foundation & User Authentication: For Landlord/Agent accounts.
Epic 2: Property Search & Listings Management: Properties listed for rent will be part of the portfolio.
Epic 3: User Dashboards & Profile Management: Landlord/Agent dashboards will house these property management tools.
Epic 4: Service Provider Marketplace & Integration: For assigning maintenance jobs to service providers.
Epic 5: Communication & Collaboration Platform: For communication with tenants regarding applications, maintenance, etc.
Epic 8: Financial Tools & Transaction Management: Will build upon the financial tracking here with online payments and e-signatures.
UI/UX designs for portfolio views, tenant management, maintenance tracking, and financial logging interfaces.
7. Technical Considerations
Data Model: Robust data model for linking properties, tenants, leases, maintenance jobs, financials, and documents.
Security & Privacy: Strict adherence to GDPR for handling tenant PII and financial data. Secure document storage with appropriate access controls.
Workflow Management: Design clear workflows for maintenance requests (from reporting to resolution) and tenant onboarding.
Reminder System: Implement a reliable background job or scheduling mechanism for document renewal and other reminders.
Scalability: The system should handle landlords/agents with potentially large property portfolios.
Integration Points: Ensure smooth integration with the Service Provider Marketplace (Epic 4) and Communication Platform (Epic 5).
Reporting: Even for basic financial overviews, ensure queries are performant.
8. Design & UX Notes
Property portfolio view should provide a clear snapshot of all properties and their statuses.
Tenant and maintenance management interfaces should be intuitive and allow for quick updates.
Document upload and organization should be simple.
Financial logging should be quick and easy.
Reminders and compliance information should be clearly presented and actionable.
Consider the different needs of individual landlords vs. estate agents managing multiple portfolios.
9. QA & Testing Strategy
Unit Tests: For financial calculation logic (if any), form validations, reminder generation logic.
Integration Tests: Test integration with service provider assignment, document storage, and communication triggers.
End-to-End (E2E) Tests:
Landlord adds a property, onboards a tenant, logs rent, creates a maintenance request, assigns it to a provider, and marks it complete.
Test document upload, renewal reminder generation, and compliance checklist usage.
Manual Testing:
Verify all property management workflows for both individual landlords and agents.
Check accuracy of financial summaries and document tracking.
Test usability of all forms and dashboards related to property management.
Ensure compliance features correctly reflect basic UK rental regulations.
Ensure all user stories within this Epic meet their individual acceptance criteria.
